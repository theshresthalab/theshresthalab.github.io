Teaching

 

 

Science makes advances based on critical thinking and evidence-based reasoning. As a teacher, I aim to communicate my enthusiasm for science, for the scientific method, and for evidence-based reasoning. Critical thinking and evidence-based reasoning are crucial to solving problems, generating ideas, and making policies and decisions. I want students to use the skills acquired in the classroom to secure employment, but more importantly, to apply those skills in the service of humanity.

Courses I have taught

  • Fall 2022: PHY211 (Modern Physics) and PHY323 (Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics)
  • Spring 2023: PHY252 (Scientific Modeling and Data Analysis) and PHY324 (Electricity and Magnetism)
  • Fall 2023: PHY321 (Classical Mechanics) and PHY101 (College Physics)
